d4rkAvatarOptimizer ships two diagnostic windows that help you understand exactly why materials won't merge and how to improve texture quality.

## Why No Material Merge

Open this window via **Tools > d4rkpl4y3r > Why No Material Merge**.

The window explains precisely why two selected material slots cannot be merged by the optimizer, making it much faster to resolve blockers than reading through the full Debug Info section.

### How to use it

1. Open the window from the menu.
2. Select your avatar in the scene.
3. Choose two material slots from the dropdowns in the window.
4. The window displays the exact reasons those slots are incompatible.

### Avatar auto-selection

The window has a toggle button that automatically selects the avatar when you click on objects in the scene. Enable it to avoid manually switching the avatar field as you inspect different meshes.

<Note>
  Results reflect the pre-build state of the avatar. Material incompatibilities introduced by VRCFury or ModularAvatar at build time will not appear here.
</Note>

## Texture Compression Analyzer

Open this window via **Tools > d4rkpl4y3r > Texture Compression Analyzer**.

This window audits the textures on your avatar and flags compression quality issues.

### What it shows

- **SSIM comparison** — a structural similarity score comparing the original texture against its compressed version, so you can quantify compression quality loss.
- **BC5 normal map suggestions** — highlights textures used as normal maps that are not already in BC5 format. BC5 provides better quality than DXT5 or BC7 at the same VRAM size.
- **Auto-convert button** — converts all flagged normal maps to BC5 in one click.

<Tip>
  Running the Texture Compression Analyzer after an initial optimize pass is a quick way to catch the remaining normal map quality issues surfaced under [NonBC5 Normal Maps](/debug/debug-info#nonbc5-normal-maps) in the Debug Info section.
</Tip>
