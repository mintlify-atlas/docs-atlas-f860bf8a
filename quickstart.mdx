## Add the component

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select your avatar root">
    In the Unity **Hierarchy**, select the root GameObject of your avatar — the one with the `VRC Avatar Descriptor` component. You can also place the component on any ancestor of the avatar root.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add d4rkAvatarOptimizer">
    In the **Inspector**, click **Add Component** and search for `d4rkAvatarOptimizer`. Select it to add the component.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  The component must be on the avatar root or an ancestor of it. Placing it on a child GameObject will have no effect.
</Note>

## Choose a preset

Select a preset that matches your avatar's complexity.

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Basic" icon="shield" href="/presets/basic">
    Safe for any avatar. Only applies optimizations that do not change visible behavior. Best for avatars over 150k triangles or when stability matters most.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Shader Toggles" icon="layers" href="/presets/shader-toggles">
    Merges meshes that toggle independently by injecting logic into shaders. Best for avatars under 150k triangles using supported shaders like Poiyomi or Silent Crosstone.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Full" icon="bolt" href="/presets/full">
    Most aggressive optimization. May change avatar behavior with blocked shaders or animations. Thorough in-editor testing is required before uploading.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Tip>
  If you are unsure which preset to use, start with **Basic**. It is always safe and produces meaningful reductions in draw calls and material count.
</Tip>

## Run the optimization

There are two workflows. Choose the one that fits your situation.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Apply on Upload" icon="upload" href="/settings/mesh-merging#apply-on-upload">
    Non-destructive. The optimizer runs automatically just before VRChat uploads your avatar. Your scene is never modified — the original stays intact.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Create Optimized Copy" icon="copy" href="/guides/troubleshooting">
    Creates a separate optimized copy of the avatar in your scene and disables the original. Use this to test the result in the editor before uploading.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

### Apply on Upload

This is the default workflow. The **Apply on Upload** toggle is enabled on the component by default.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Confirm Apply on Upload is enabled">
    On the `d4rkAvatarOptimizer` component, verify that **Apply on Upload** is checked.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Upload via the VRChat SDK">
    Open the VRChat SDK panel (**VRChat SDK → Show Control Panel**) and upload your avatar as normal. The optimizer runs automatically before the upload is sent.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Your original avatar in the scene is never changed. Every upload triggers a fresh optimization pass from the unmodified source.
</Note>

### Create Optimized Copy

Use this workflow to inspect and validate the optimized avatar before uploading.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click Create Optimized Copy">
    On the `d4rkAvatarOptimizer` component, click the **Create Optimized Copy** button. The optimizer creates a new GameObject with the optimized avatar and disables the original.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Inspect the result">
    Select the optimized copy in the Hierarchy and check the mesh and material counts in the Inspector. Enter Play Mode or use the VRChat SDK panel to preview the avatar.

    <Warning>
      Do not edit the optimized copy. It is generated from scratch each time you click the button, so any manual changes will be lost. Make all edits on the original avatar.
    </Warning>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Upload or discard">
    If everything looks correct, upload the optimized copy using the VRChat SDK. When you are done, delete the copy — it is not meant to be kept in your scene long-term.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What to expect after optimization

After running the optimizer, a well-configured avatar typically shows:

- **Fewer skinned mesh renderers** — meshes that are always animated together are merged into one
- **Fewer material slots** — identical material slots are combined; with Shader Toggles enabled, materials sharing the same shader are merged even when their properties differ
- **A leaner FX layer** — simple toggle layers are collapsed into a single direct blend tree
- **Smaller upload size** — baked blend shapes and static shader values reduce asset size

The exact savings depend on how many meshes and materials your avatar has, which preset you chose, and whether your shaders are compatible with the optimizer.
